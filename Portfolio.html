No lockdown, no recession?

My sixthteenth chart looks at Danish and Swedish growth rates:

Reflecting on Flavio Toxvaerd answer to lockdown being the best solution to Covid-19. By comparing Sweden which actively promoted an anti-lockdown policy and Denmark which was one of the first countries to implement lockdown measures, the economic costs are similar.

It would seem that forcing people to remain home would have an economic cost larger than keeping the economy open, we would expect Swedes to continue to go to work and consume goods, whereas Danes would stay at home, not spending any money in hospitality and national tourism.

The data suggests that Sweden suffered a stronger recession than its Nordic counterpart in the second quarter of 2020, both economies quickly recovering in the next quarter. As Sweden is an export-oriented economy, lockdown measures imposed by other countries impacted its economic growth. Thus the easing out of lockdown measures in the summer of 2020 permitted growth in both countries.

From an economic perspective lockdown measure in Denmark didn’t increase the economic cost in the short term. However, social and mental cost for Sweden and Denmark’s response in the long run would need to be analysed.
